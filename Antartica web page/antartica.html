Antartica in December

December is a popular month for Antarctic travel as the warmer temperatures and increasingly long days breathe life back into the region. Wildlife numbers are increasing and hungry humpback whales start arriving. Look out for the first penguin chicks which start to hatch.

5 Reasons to Visit Antarctica in December

  1. The start of summer - up to 22 hours of daylight and sunnier days means more time for exploring
  2. The density of wildlife is increasing - penguin rookeries are at full capacity, while seal and whale sightings become common
  3. Penguin chicks begin to hatch, first in the Falklands, and then from mid-December on the Peninsula
  4. December marks the arrival back in Antarctic waters of hungry humpback whales from tropical waters
  5. Access to the landing sites becomes easier in December and breakup of ice allows wider exploration

Our Trips in Antartica

Cruise to the Antarctic Peninsula

Sail through icy waters, surrounded by towering glaciers and stunning icebergs.Experience the thrill of close encounters with wildlife and capture breathtaking moments as the ship navigates through the pristine Antarctic landscapes.

Wildlife Extravaganza:

Immerse yourself in the vibrant Antarctic ecosystem as you witness penguins, seals, and seabirds in their natural habitats. Our expert guides will provide insights into their behavior and breeding cycles.

Zodiac Cruising and Iceberg Encounters:

Glide through icy waters on Zodiac boats, navigating close to stunning ice formations and wildlife-rich coastal areas. Capture breathtaking moments as our guides share their knowledge of the region.

Exclusive Research Station Visit:

Gain a firsthand understanding of Antarctic research at active stations. Interact with scientists, learn about ongoing projects, and see how researchers live and work in this extreme environment.

Aurora Australis Night:

Experience the enchanting Southern Lights during a dedicated night for aurora viewing. Marvel at the celestial dance of colors across the Antarctic sky, a truly magical and unforgettable spectacle.

Christmas At Antartica:

A fantastic time to visit the 7th continent, for a guaranteed white Christmas! Enjoy festive hosting onboard your expedition vessel, as well as plenty of wildlife sightings to keep your experience magical. The weather is excellent in the festive time period, and with penguin chicks beginning to hatch, you'll certainly have lots of photographs to treasure your time at the end of the world.
